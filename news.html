  • (2026.5) A systematic literature review, Quantum Optimization for Software Engineering: A Survey, has just been accepted by ACM Transactions on Software Engineering and Methodology (TOSEM). This survey comprehensively reviews the methodologies of applying quantum-inspired and quantum optimization algorithms to handle software engineering problems and outlines potential research directions in this emerging area.
  • (2026.3) A new study, A Dynamic Test Oracle for Quantum Programs with Separable Output States, has been accepted for publication in IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering (TSE). This study systematically investigates the test oracle problem for quantum programs, and provides a feasible solution for handling curse of dimensionality in quantum software testing. One of the empirical insights is that using hypothesis tests and statistical measures as test oracles for quantum software is not always reliable, as the sensitivity to parameter configurations (e.g., shots and thresholds) and the association with inherent limitations (e.g., challenge in handling the curse of dimensionality and observing the relative phase).
