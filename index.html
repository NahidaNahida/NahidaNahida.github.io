    Yuechen Li

    Yuechen Li (李玥辰)

    GitHub | Google Scholar | ORCID

    Yuechen is a four-year Ph.D. candidate with Beihang University. His interests include both theoretical and empirical research on foundations of quantum software engineering (QSE), and applications of quantum information theory /quantum computing. The current studies during his Ph.D. program have a focus on quantum software testing.

    News

    • (2026.5) A systematic literature review Quantum Optimization for Software Engineering: A Survey has just been accepted by ACM Transactions on Software Engineering and Methodology (TOSEM). This survey comprehensively reviews the methodologies of applying quantum-inspired and quantum optimization algorithms to handle software engineering problems and outlines potential research directions in this emerging area.
    • (2026.3) A new study A Dynamic Test Oracle for Quantum Programs with Separable Output States has been accepted for publication in IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering (TSE). This study systematically investigates the test oracle problem for quantum programs, and provides a feasible solution for handling curse of dimensionality in quantum software testing. One of the empirical insights is that using hypothesis tests and statistical measures as test oracles for quantum software are not reliable, which is sensitive to parameter configurations and associated with inherent limitations (e.g., struggle to handle the curse of dimensionality and observe the phase).
